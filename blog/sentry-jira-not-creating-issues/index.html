Why Sentry isn't creating Jira issues automatically (and how to fix it)

Setup guide · updated July 2026

You connected Sentry to Jira, expected new errors to show up as tickets, and… nothing happens. Errors keep piling up in Sentry, the backlog stays empty, and you're stuck copy-pasting. If you searched "Sentry not creating Jira issues" to find out whether you misconfigured something — mostly, you didn't. Here's what's actually going on.

1. Auto-creation is gated behind paid Sentry plans

Sentry's native Jira integration can create issues from an alert rule — you add a Create a new Jira issue action to an Issue Alert. But there's a catch buried in the docs: automatic issue creation is only available on Business and Enterprise plans. On the Free and Team plans you can link an issue to Jira by hand from the Sentry UI, but errors won't turn into tickets on their own.

Quick check: if your "Create a Jira issue" alert action doesn't fire, and you're on Free or Team, that's why. It's a plan limit, not a misconfiguration.

2. The official Marketplace app has a rough reputation

Separately from the native integration, there's an official Sentry for Jira app on the Atlassian Marketplace. At the time of writing it sits at roughly 2.5★ across ~100 reviews. The recurring complaints are consistent: the configuration screen fails to load, and issues silently don't get created even after setup looks complete. If you installed that and nothing happened, you're not alone.

3. Even when it works, regressions fall through

The part almost every setup misses: you fix the bug, close the Jira ticket, and three weeks later the same error resurfaces in Sentry. Nothing reopens the ticket. The regression just sits in Sentry unnoticed until someone happens to look.

What are your options?

Option A — Upgrade your Sentry plan

Moving to Business unlocks native auto-creation. If you're already heading there for other reasons, it's the least-effort path. If you're upgrading only to get issues into Jira, it's an expensive way to solve one workflow — Business pricing is per-org and adds up fast next to the actual need.

Option B — Build it yourself on a webhook

Every Sentry plan can send the issue webhook. You can point it at a small service that creates a Jira issue via the Jira REST API. This works, but you're now maintaining a server, handling deduplication (so one error doesn't spawn a thousand tickets), mapping statuses, and dealing with regressions yourself. It's a weekend project that becomes a thing you own forever.

Option C — Use a purpose-built bridge

This is the gap we built Sentry Bridge for Jira to fill. It listens on the issue webhook — available on any Sentry plan — and turns each error into a Jira issue with the culprit, level, environment, event count and a link back to Sentry. It deduplicates (one issue per error, no floods), and when a closed issue's error comes back it reopens the ticket and comments with the current event count. It runs entirely inside Atlassian as a Forge app — no servers of yours, no data leaving your tenant.

We're deliberate about limits: the issue.created webhook every plan can use carries the facts (culprit, level, environment, count, permalink) but not the stack trace. If your plan allows the Errors webhook resource, the exception and top frames come through too. Either way the "Open in Sentry" link goes straight to the full trace. We'd rather say that up front than surprise you.
